# Air Fryer Probe — Target Audience

## Primary audience

**UK home cooks who own or are switching to an air fryer.**

They are not professional chefs. They cook regularly for themselves and their families. They have experienced the frustration of air fryer cooking: faster cook times than recipes suggest, dry chicken, uncertainty about whether meat is safe and juicy.

### Demographics (inferred from site and testimonials)

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| Geography | United Kingdom (testimonials from UK, Newbury, Bournemouth, London) |
| Age | Broad adult range; site has 18+ age gate |
| Cooking skill | Beginner to enthusiastic home cook — not necessarily thermometer users before |
| Device ownership | Air fryer (primary); may also BBQ, grill, or use convection oven |
| Purchase trigger | Gift (Christmas mentioned), personal frustration with cooking results, recommendation, realising their air fryer overcooks food faster than a conventional oven — often despite following instructions |

### Psychographics

- **Anxious about doneness** — cuts into meat to check, or overcooks "to be safe"
- **Recipe-frustrated** — air fryer times don't match oven recipes
- **Blindly overcooking** — follows food packaging timings but still gets dry results; doesn't know those timings are calibrated for conventional ovens, not their high-powered air fryer
- **Practical, not aspirational** — wants a tool that works, not a lifestyle brand
- **Value-conscious** — £23.99 price point; responds to bundle value (free hook + guide)
- **Convenience-driven** — loves the hook keeping probe at the air fryer
- **Trust-seeking** — responds to professional credibility (25 years in catering) and customer reviews

## Jobs to be done

1. **"Is my chicken actually cooked?"** — Need certainty without destroying the meal
2. **"Why is my air fryer food dry?"** — Overcooking because they can't trust timing
3. **"What temperature should this be?"** — Don't know internal temp targets for different proteins
4. **"Where did I put the probe?"** — Need it accessible when cooking, not buried in a drawer
5. **"Can I trust this cheap gadget?"** — Need proof: waterproof, fast, accurate, solid feel

## Pain points the brand solves

| Pain | Brand response |
|---|---|
| Air fryers cook faster than recipes — and faster than conventional ovens; food packaging timings are calibrated for the weakest ovens, not high-powered air fryers | Cook to internal temp, not time |
| Narrow window between perfect and overcooked (under 2 minutes between 74°C and 80°C) — air fryer heat is less forgiving than a conventional oven | 0.6s probe reading lets you pull food at the exact right moment |
| Don't know safe internal temperatures | Free magnetic Goldilocks chart |
| Probe gets lost in kitchen drawer | Self-adhesive hook on air fryer |
| Previous probes too small to read | 50% larger LCD screen |
| Fear of washing electronics | IP67 waterproof |
| Cutting meat open to check | Insert probe, read temp, done |

## Audience segments

### 1. The Air Fryer Convert
Recently bought an air fryer. Frustrated that oven recipes don't work. Looking for confidence.

**Message angle:** "Packaging times were written for conventional ovens. Air fryers cook faster — and food goes from perfectly cooked to ruined in under two minutes."

### 2. The Cautious Cook
Cooks meat but always overcooks to be safe. Dry chicken is normal.

**Message angle:** "Hit the temperature. Pull it out. Done."

### 3. The Gift Recipient
Received probe as Christmas gift. Discovered they can't stop using it.

**Message angle:** Social proof, testimonial-led. "Game changer — no more dry chicken."

### 4. The Upgrade Buyer
Has used probes before. Wants better: bigger screen, waterproof, faster reading.

**Message angle:** Spec-led. "0.6 second reading. IP67 waterproof. 50% larger display."

### 5. The Multi-Use Cook
Uses air fryer primarily but also BBQs, grills, checks baby milk.

**Message angle:** "Designed for air fryers. Works everywhere you cook."

## What they respond to

- **Numbers and specs** — ±0.5°C, 0.6s, IP67, −50°C to 300°C
- **Before/after outcomes** — dry chicken → juicy chicken
- **Social proof** — named UK testimonials with specific outcomes
- **Bundle value** — free hook, free guide, batteries included
- **Simplicity** — "just so handy", "easy to use", "key bit of kit"

## What they don't respond to

- Chef-exclusive language or technique gatekeeping
- Lifestyle aspirational photography (staged perfect kitchens)
- Hype words: revolutionary, game-changing in brand-written copy (OK in customer quotes). Ultimate is allowed.
- Complexity — multiple steps, jargon, rhetorical questions in hero copy

- Professional/commercial kitchens (though pro technique is referenced)
