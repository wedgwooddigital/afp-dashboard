# Air Fryer Probe — Voice and Messaging

## Voice profile

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Core tone** | Confident, direct, knowledgeable |
| **Register** | Everyday kitchen English — not chef-speak, not corporate |
| **Energy** | Calm authority. Removes anxiety; doesn't create urgency through hype. |
| **Perspective** | "We've solved this" — experienced, practical, trustworthy |
| **Sentence style** | Short declarative sentences. Fragments OK for emphasis. |

**Brand talks like:** A knowledgeable friend who cooks well and has figured out the air fryer temperature problem.

**Brand does not talk like:** A gadget infomercial, a Michelin chef, or a generic DTC brand using superlatives.

## Voice principles

### Do

- Lead with the outcome the customer wants, not the feature
- Use temperature data as proof — numbers are credible
- Acknowledge cooking anxiety: "You'll never cut into a chicken breast to check again"
- Use active voice
- Short punchy sequences: "Hit the temperature. Pull it out. Done."
- Reference professional technique accessibly: "Pro's cook to temperature not time"

### Don't

- Use banned words in brand voice: **revolutionary**, **game-changing**
  - Exception: customer testimonials may use these (e.g. "game changer" in Olivia's review)
  - **Ultimate** is allowed — client preference; common in blog/SEO titles (e.g. temperature chart post)
- Ask rhetorical questions in hero copy
- Use passive voice
- Over-promise or use filler superlatives
- Gatekeep with chef jargon

## Key messages (hierarchy)

### 1. Core promise
Perfectly cooked food every time — juicy, safe, delicious.

### 2. Problem
Air fryers cook faster than recipes expect — and faster than conventional ovens. Food packaging timings are calibrated for conventional ovens (the weakest appliances in the test); your high-powered air fryer runs ahead of those times regardless. Even between air fryers, a 1500W basket cooks faster than a 1700W unit. Timing alone fails. The window between perfectly cooked and overcooked is less than two minutes. Cutting into meat to check ruins the meal.

### 3. Solution
Measure internal temperature with a probe designed for air fryer use.

### 4. Proof points
- ±0.5°C accuracy
- 0.6 second reading
- IP67 waterproof
- 50% larger display
- 25 years in catering (see `trust-and-proof.md`)

### 5. Bundle value
Free magnetic temperature guide + free self-adhesive hook included.

### 6. Convenience
Always within reach — hangs on your air fryer.

## Brand arguments (named concepts)

Recurring persuasive structures the brand has developed and published across Facebook. Use freely as frameworks; rewrite rather than copy verbatim.

### The Doom Spiral
The thermal science of why air fryer chicken overcooks so fast once it passes the target temperature. Published Jan–Feb; most developed form in the 1 Feb post.

1. **The Squeeze** — at 75°C, muscle fibres contract and force moisture out
2. **The Acceleration** — water evaporation strips thermal mass; temperature then climbs faster and faster
3. **The Window** — less than two minutes between 74°C (Goldilocks zone) and 80°C (overcooked)

Anchor line: "You have exactly 120 seconds to save your dinner."
Three-part label to use: **Squeeze / Acceleration / Window**

### The Liability Loop
Why following food packaging instructions leads to overcooking in an air fryer. Published Apr–May.

- Packaging timings are calibrated for conventional ovens — the weakest appliances
- Air fryers are not standardised: a 1500W basket cooks faster than a 1700W unit
- Both routes fail: "If you follow instructions, you're overcooking. If you trust instincts, you're probably still overcooking."

Resolution: "Switch from time to temperature. Professional chefs don't wait for a timer; they cook to temperature."

### Objection handling — "Isn't a probe for beginners?"
Published SatNav triad from 15 Apr post. Use to reframe skill and precision:

> Using SatNav doesn't mean you can't drive.
> Using Spell Check doesn't mean you can't write.
> Using a Probe doesn't mean you can't cook.
> It means you've learnt the same lessons as every professional chef. Consistency is about temperature, not intuition.

Positioning line: "The SatNav for your kitchen."

## Emoji guidance (social)

Use sparingly. When used:
- 🌡️ temperature tips
- ✅ results / checklist
- 🍗 chicken / poultry content
- 🔥 air fryer / heat (use rarely)

Avoid emoji-heavy captions. The brand voice is confident and clean, not playful-casual.
