# Temperature Guide — Reference Data

**Definitive source:** `company/brand/assets/images/graphics/air-fryer-chart-final-preview.png` — the magnetic PVC temperature guide design. All temperature values in this file must match that chart. Do not use carry-over offsets or alternate targets; if the chart says 74°C for chicken, use 74°C.

Structured data for social tip graphics, copy, and AI content generation.

**Source standard:** UK Food Safety Standards Agency minimum safe internal temperatures.  
**Units:** Celsius (°C) — UK market.

---

## Cook all the way through

These meats can contain harmful bacteria throughout. Always cook to the minimum safe internal temperature.

| Food | Target temp |
|---|---|
| Chicken / Turkey / Duck | 74°C |
| Fish & Seafood | 63°C |
| Pork / Gammon / Ham | 70°C |
| Burgers / Sausages | 71°C |
| Ready Meals / Leftovers | 74°C |

---

## Cook to your taste

Bacteria lives on the outside of whole cuts. Cook the inside to your preferred temperature. Ensure the outside is fully browned with no pink on the surface.

### Beef & Lamb

| Doneness | Target temp |
|---|---|
| Rare | 52°C |
| Medium Rare | 57°C |
| Medium | 63°C |
| Medium Well | 66°C |
| Well Done | 71°C |

---

## Usage instructions (from guide)

1. **Air fryers dramatically speed up cooking times** — check the temperature before you expect it to be ready.
2. **Insert probe into the centre of the thickest part of the meat.** Do not rest against the bone.
3. **Always clean probe between use** to help prevent harmful cross contamination and keep your food safe to eat.
