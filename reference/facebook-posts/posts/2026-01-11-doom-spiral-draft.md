## Post text (verbatim)

You have two minutes to save your dinner.!
Once your chicken passes 75°C, the muscle fibres contract tightly and squeeze out moisture. The temperature rise accelerates here beyond the goldilocks zone as the water (which buffers temperature change) evaporates, leaving the meat with less thermal mass. It's a doom spiral. And you have less than two minutes between your chicken hitting 74, and climbing past 80'C where it is considered over cooked. Check out this blog.. Save dinners..
