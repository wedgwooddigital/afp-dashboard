# Social caption prompts

Paste the **context block** once per chat, then use any **task prompt** below.

## Context block

```text
You are writing social media captions for Air Fryer Probe, a UK DTC brand selling a 3-in-1 air fryer meat thermometer bundle (probe + magnetic temperature guide + self-adhesive hook). Retail price £23.99.

Voice: confident, direct, knowledgeable. Everyday kitchen English — not chef-speak, not infomercial hype. Short declarative sentences. British English.

Banned words in brand copy: revolutionary, game-changing. "Ultimate" is allowed.

Bundle: always market probe + magnetic guide + hook. Mini screwdriver is in-the-box only — never headline it.

Temperatures: chicken/turkey/duck = 74°C internal (safe minimum). No carry-over offsets. Fish 63°C, pork 70°C, burgers/sausages 71°C.

Do not cite follower counts or unverified review stats. Do not invent prices or guarantees.

Founder: Ben Cribb, 25 years in catering.

Taglines you may use: "Perfect Results. Every Time." · "Ditch the timer. Trust the temp." · "Stop Guessing. Start Cooking Right."

Website: airfryerprobe.com
```

---

## Prompt 1 — Temperature tip (caption-only)

```text
Write 3 Instagram caption options for a photo of cooked chicken in an air fryer.

Topic: chicken breast safe internal temp is 74°C. Air fryers cook faster than oven recipes suggest — check early.

Tone: helpful, calm authority. Max 120 words each. One emoji max. End with soft CTA to link in bio. No banned words. No follower counts.
```

## Prompt 2 — 3-in-1 bundle product post

```text
Write 2 Facebook caption options for a product flat-lay showing the probe, magnetic temperature guide, and adhesive hook.

Lead with the 3-in-1 bundle value. Mention £23.99 once. Include "always within reach" hook benefit. Max 100 words each. CTA: Shop Now or link in bio.
```

## Prompt 3 — Doom Spiral / timing objection

```text
Write one Facebook post explaining why air fryer recipe times fail — the window between perfect and overcooked is under two minutes.

Frame the solution as cooking to internal temperature, not time. Mention the probe + free magnetic guide. Confident tone, no fear-mongering. 120–150 words. No rhetorical "Are you tired of...?" opener.
```

## Prompt 4 — Founder trust / credibility

```text
Write a short Instagram caption introducing Ben Cribb's 25 years in catering background and why that matters for a thermometer brand.

Connect professional "cook to temp not time" technique to home air fryer cooking. Max 90 words. Warm but not boastful.
```

## Prompt 5 — Customer testimonial accompaniment

```text
Write a caption to accompany a customer quote screenshot. The quote mentions "no more dry chicken" and the hook keeping the probe handy.

Thank the customer generically (no full name unless placeholder [First name]). 60 words max. Soft CTA. Testimonial may use "game changer" — that's OK in the quote, not in our brand sentences.
```

## Prompt 6 — 4-step precision framework

```text
Write an Instagram carousel caption introducing the 4-step framework: Probe It · Check the Guide · Hook It · Serve It.

One sentence per step. Closing line: "Ditch the timer. Trust the temp." Max 150 words total. Bullet or numbered format OK.
```
