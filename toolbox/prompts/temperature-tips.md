# Temperature tip prompts

Educational posts for air fryer audiences. **All values from the magnetic temperature guide** — chicken = **74°C** safe minimum. No carry-over offsets.

## Context block

```text
You are writing temperature-tip content for Air Fryer Probe (UK DTC thermometer brand).

Use Celsius only. Safe minimum internal temps: chicken/turkey/duck 74°C, fish 63°C, pork 70°C, burgers/sausages 71°C, ready meals 74°C.

Voice: confident, direct. No "revolutionary" or "game-changing". British English.

If designing a card: protein name in Rubik, temp in Roboto Mono Bold, Dark Navy (#11151C) background, Brand Orange (#FD9E27) for headings only.
```

---

## Prompt 1 — Chicken breast (primary)

```text
Write a temperature tip for chicken breast in an air fryer.

Card copy: protein name + "74°C" + one line "internal temp · safe min · check early in air fryer".

Caption: explain air fryers cook faster than oven recipes — check early. Max 100 words. One emoji max.
```

## Prompt 2 — Sausages & burgers

```text
Write a temperature tip for sausages or burgers in an air fryer. Safe minimum 71°C internal.

Include why timing alone fails in air fryers. Caption max 90 words. CTA: magnetic guide included with probe.
```

## Prompt 3 — Fish fillet

```text
Write a short temperature tip for white fish in an air fryer. Safe minimum 63°C internal.

Keep it practical — flaky test vs temp. Max 80 words.
```

## Prompt 4 — Multi-protein carousel script

```text
Write an Instagram carousel script (5 slides) listing safe internal temps for: chicken (74°C), pork (70°C), sausages (71°C), fish (63°C).

Slide 1: hook — "Recipe times lie. Internal temp doesn't." Slides 2–5: one protein each. Final slide: CTA to shop the 3-in-1 bundle. Bullet points only, max 25 words per slide.
```

## Prompt 5 — Doom Spiral framing

```text
Write a Facebook post using the "120-second window" concept: once chicken passes 74°C in a hot air fryer, it can go from perfect to dry very quickly.

Resolution: pull at temp, not at timer. Mention the magnetic guide on the fridge. 120–140 words. No fear-mongering.
```
